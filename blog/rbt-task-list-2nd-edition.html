Section A: Foundations (12%)

This section covers the basic principles of ABA that everything else builds on. Think of it as the vocabulary of behavior analysis.

Key Topics:

  • Reinforcement ‚Äî positive vs. negative, schedules of reinforcement
  • Punishment ‚Äî positive vs. negative (remember: positive = adding, negative = removing)
  • Extinction ‚Äî withholding reinforcement
  • Motivating operations ‚Äî establishing operations (EO) and abolishing operations (AO)
  • Stimulus control ‚Äî discriminative stimuli, stimulus generalization
  • Three-term contingency ‚Äî antecedent, behavior, consequence

Study tip: Make sure you can explain the difference between positive/negative reinforcement AND positive/negative punishment. This trips up a lot of people. Remember: positive/negative = add/remove. Reinforcement/punishment = behavior increases/decreases.

Section B: Assessment (12%)

This section is about collecting and measuring data. As an RBT, data collection is a huge part of your daily job.

Key Topics:

  • Data collection methods ‚Äî frequency, duration, latency, interval recording, permanent product
  • Preference assessments ‚Äî single stimulus, paired stimulus, multiple stimulus (with and without replacement)
  • ABC data ‚Äî antecedent-behavior-consequence recording
  • Graphing ‚Äî line graphs, x-axis vs. y-axis conventions
  • Interobserver agreement (IOA) ‚Äî measuring reliability between observers

Study tip: Know when to use each data collection method. Duration = how long. Latency = how long before it starts. Frequency = how many times. Interval = did it happen during this time chunk?

Section C: Skill Acquisition (28%)

This is the biggest section on the exam ‚Äî over a quarter of all questions. It covers how to teach new skills.

Key Topics:

  • Discrete Trial Training (DTT) ‚Äî structured teaching with clear trials
  • Natural Environment Teaching (NET) ‚Äî teaching during natural activities
  • Prompting ‚Äî types (physical, model, gestural, verbal, visual) and the prompt hierarchy
  • Prompt fading ‚Äî most-to-least, least-to-most, time delay
  • Chaining ‚Äî forward, backward, total task
  • Shaping ‚Äî reinforcing successive approximations
  • Task analysis ‚Äî breaking complex skills into steps
  • Generalization and maintenance ‚Äî skills across settings and over time
  • Token economies ‚Äî conditioned reinforcement systems
  • Behavior momentum ‚Äî high-probability request sequences

Study tip: This section requires both knowledge AND application. Expect scenario-based questions like "A child can wash hands at home but not at school ‚Äî what is this a problem with?" (Answer: generalization). Practice applying concepts to real situations.

Section D: Behavior Reduction (12%)

This section covers reducing problem behaviors using function-based strategies.

Key Topics:

  • Functions of behavior ‚Äî attention, escape, tangible, sensory (ACES/SEAT)
  • Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA) ‚Äî understanding why behavior occurs
  • Antecedent interventions ‚Äî modifying the environment to prevent behavior
  • Differential reinforcement ‚Äî DRA, DRO, DRI, DRL
  • Extinction ‚Äî and extinction bursts
  • Functional Communication Training (FCT) ‚Äî teaching replacement communication

Study tip: Know the four functions cold. Every behavior reduction question starts with identifying the function. Also understand extinction bursts ‚Äî the temporary increase in behavior when you first stop reinforcing it.

Section E: Documentation and Reporting (12%)

This section is about accurate record-keeping and communication with your team.

Key Topics:

  • Session notes ‚Äî objective, accurate, timely documentation
  • Data reporting ‚Äî accurate and honest data recording
  • Graphing conventions ‚Äî proper labeling, axes, phase change lines
  • Communication ‚Äî reporting changes in behavior to your BCBA
  • Confidentiality ‚Äî HIPAA, secure storage of records

Study tip: The key principle here is objectivity. Use observable, measurable language. "Client was angry" ‚ùå vs. "Client threw 3 items and screamed for 2 minutes" ‚úì.

Section F: Professional Conduct and Scope of Practice (24%)

The second-biggest section. Almost a quarter of the exam is about ethics and professional behavior.

Key Topics:

  • Scope of practice ‚Äî what RBTs can and cannot do
  • Supervision requirements ‚Äî working under a BCBA
  • Professional boundaries ‚Äî dual relationships, gifts, social media
  • Mandatory reporting ‚Äî abuse, neglect, ethical violations
  • Client dignity ‚Äî respect, least restrictive interventions
  • Cultural sensitivity ‚Äî respecting diverse backgrounds
  • Ethical violations ‚Äî when and how to report
  • Certification maintenance ‚Äî annual renewal, competency assessments

Study tip: When in doubt on an ethics question, ask yourself: "What protects the client?" The answer that prioritizes client welfare, follows the BCBA's plan, and maintains professional boundaries is almost always correct.

üí° Golden Rule for Ethics Questions: If an answer says "the RBT should decide on their own" or "modify the plan independently," it's almost certainly wrong. RBTs always defer to their supervising BCBA.

Current RBT Exam Pass Rate

According to data published by the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB), the RBT exam pass rate has historically ranged between 68% and 80%, depending on the year and candidate population.

Key statistics:

  • First-attempt pass rate: Approximately 72‚Äì75%

  • Total RBTs certified worldwide: Over 120,000 (as of 2024)

Data-Driven Study Strategies

Based on the pass rate data and exam structure, here are the most effective strategies:

1. Prioritize Professional Conduct (36% of the exam)

This is the single largest content area. Study the BACB Ethics Code for Behavior Analysts and understand RBT scope of practice. Practice scenario-based questions where you must choose the most ethical response.

2. Master the Terminology

Many questions hinge on understanding precise ABA definitions. Use our ABA glossary as a study reference and make flashcards for terms you struggle with.

3. Take Practice Exams Under Timed Conditions

The RBT exam gives you 90 minutes for 85 questions ‚Äî about 1 minute per question. Practice under timed conditions to build speed and confidence. Try our free practice questions.

4. Study the RBT Task List (2nd Edition) Systematically

Go through each task on the RBT Task List and make sure you can explain it in your own words with an example. If you can't, study that area more.

5. Follow a Study Schedule

A structured 4-week study plan is more effective than random studying. Dedicate more time to high-weight areas (professional conduct, skill acquisition) and less to areas you're already strong in.

6. Use Spaced Repetition

Don't cram. Study a little every day and review previously learned material at increasing intervals. This technique is proven to improve long-term retention.

What If You Don't Pass?

Failing the RBT exam is not the end of the road. Here's what you need to know:

  • Retake policy: You can take the exam up to 8 times within your 2-year authorization period
  • Waiting period: 30 days after the first failed attempt before retaking
  • Cost: Each retake requires a new exam fee ($45 as of 2024)
  • Retake pass rate: Candidates who retake the exam after additional preparation have a higher pass rate (~80%+)

Steps After a Failed Attempt

  1. Review your score report ‚Äî The BACB provides feedback on your performance by content area
  2. Identify weak areas ‚Äî Focus your studying on the sections where you scored lowest
  3. Take more practice exams ‚Äî Familiarize yourself with the question style
  4. Consider additional training ‚Äî Review materials or take a refresher course
  5. Schedule your retake ‚Äî Don't wait too long; study while the material is fresh

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current RBT exam pass rate?

The RBT exam first-attempt pass rate is approximately 72‚Äì75%. This means roughly 1 in 4 candidates do not pass on their first try. The overall pass rate (including retakes) is higher, around 78‚Äì82%.

How many questions do you need to get right to pass the RBT exam?

The exam has 85 multiple-choice questions: 75 scored and 10 unscored pilot questions (you won't know which are which). The BACB uses a criterion-referenced passing standard, but you generally need to answer approximately 56‚Äì60 of the 75 scored questions correctly (roughly 75‚Äì80%).

How many times can you retake the RBT exam?

You can retake the exam up to 8 times within a single 2-year authorization period. There's a mandatory 30-day waiting period after the first failed attempt. Each retake requires a new exam fee.

Is the RBT exam harder than other certification exams?

The RBT exam is moderately difficult. Its 72‚Äì75% pass rate is comparable to the CNA exam (75‚Äì83%) and significantly higher than the BCBA exam (57‚Äì65%). The exam tests applied knowledge and ethical judgment, which can be challenging without hands-on experience.

What topics are most commonly missed on the RBT exam?

The most challenging areas tend to be: measurement and data collection (different recording methods), behavior reduction procedures (knowing when to use specific strategies), and professional conduct/scope of practice (ethical judgment scenarios). The professional conduct section is the largest at 36% of the exam.

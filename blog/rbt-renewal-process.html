Renewal Requirements Overview

The BACB requires the following for annual RBT renewal:

RequirementDetails
FrequencyAnnually (every 12 months)
Competency assessmentAnnual re-assessment by a BCBA/BCaBA
Ongoing supervisionMinimum 5% of hours spent providing ABA services
Renewal fee$35/year
Continuing educationNot required by BACB (but recommended)
Background checkNot required annually (only at initial certification)

Step 1: Complete Ongoing Supervision

Throughout your certification year, you must receive ongoing supervision from a qualified supervisor (BCBA, BCaBA under BCBA oversight, or BCBA-D):

  • Supervision must equal at least 5% of the total hours you spend providing ABA services
  • Supervision must include direct observation of you working with clients
  • Supervision contacts must occur at least twice per month
  • At least one supervision contact per month must include direct observation (in person or via telehealth)
  • Your supervisor must document all supervision contacts

Example: If you work 120 hours per month providing ABA services, you need at least 6 hours of supervision per month (120 √ó 5% = 6 hours).

Step 2: Pass Annual Competency Assessment

Before renewing, you must complete a new competency assessment. This is similar to the initial competency assessment you completed when first getting certified:

  • Must be conducted by your supervising BCBA, BCaBA, or BCBA-D
  • Uses the BACB's standardized RBT Competency Assessment form
  • Covers all areas of the RBT Task List
  • You must be rated "competent" on all items
  • Must be completed within the certification cycle (before your expiration date)

Step 3: Submit Renewal Application

To submit your renewal:

  1. Log in to your BACB account at bacb.com
  2. Navigate to your certification management page
  3. Confirm your competency assessment is on file
  4. Attest that you've met all supervision requirements
  5. Complete the renewal application form

Step 4: Pay the Renewal Fee

The RBT annual renewal fee is $35, payable online through the BACB portal. Payment is required to complete the renewal process.

Important Deadlines

Your renewal deadline is based on your initial certification date:

  • The BACB sends email reminders 90 days, 60 days, and 30 days before your expiration date
  • You can submit your renewal as early as 60 days before your expiration date
  • There is a 30-day grace period after expiration, but you cannot practice as an RBT during this period
  • After the grace period, your certification is officially lapsed

Pro tip: Set a calendar reminder for 90 days before your expiration date. Don't rely solely on BACB emails.

What If Your Certification Lapses?

If you miss the renewal deadline and grace period:

  • You cannot practice as an RBT or represent yourself as certified
  • If it's been less than 30 days past expiration: you may still renew with a late fee
  • If it's been more than 30 days: you may need to complete the full certification process again (training, competency assessment, background check, exam)
  • Contact the BACB directly if you're in a lapse situation ‚Äî they can advise on your specific case

Tips for a Smooth Renewal

  1. Track your supervision hours throughout the year ‚Äî don't scramble at renewal time
  2. Schedule your competency assessment early ‚Äî at least 2 months before your expiration date
  3. Keep your BACB account information current ‚Äî especially your email address
  4. Set multiple calendar reminders for your renewal deadline
  5. Communicate with your supervisor ‚Äî make sure they know your renewal timeline
  6. Keep copies of all supervision documentation ‚Äî the BACB may audit you

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do you need to renew your RBT certification?

RBT certification must be renewed annually. The certification cycle runs for one year from the date of initial certification or last renewal.

What is the RBT renewal fee?

The RBT renewal fee is $35 per year, paid to the BACB through their online portal.

What happens if my RBT certification expires?

If your RBT certification expires, you cannot practice as an RBT. You may be able to apply for reinstatement within 30 days of expiration. After 30 days, you may need to go through the full certification process again, including a new background check and exam.

Do RBTs need continuing education?

The BACB does not require continuing education units (CEUs) for RBT renewal. However, RBTs must complete an annual competency assessment and maintain ongoing supervision by a BCBA.

Can I renew my RBT if I'm not currently working?

This can be challenging. You need to have completed supervision requirements and a competency assessment. If you're not currently working in ABA, talk to a BCBA who may be willing to supervise you and conduct the assessment.

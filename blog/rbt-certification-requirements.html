RBT Certification Requirements: Step-by-Step Guide (2025)

üìÖ February 2025 ‚è± 10 min read üìã Certification

The Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) is an entry-level certification in applied behavior analysis (ABA) issued by the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB). With over 120,000 active RBTs in the US, it's one of the most accessible and in-demand healthcare certifications available.

Here's exactly what you need to do to become an RBT in 2025.

Step 1: Meet Eligibility Requirements

Before starting the certification process, confirm you meet the basic eligibility criteria:

  • You must be at least 18 years old
  • You must have a high school diploma or GED
  • No college degree is required
  • No prior experience in ABA is required (though helpful)

Step 2: Complete 40-Hour RBT Training

You must complete a 40-hour training program that covers the content areas of the RBT Task List (currently the 2nd Edition). The training must:

  • Be based on the BACB's RBT Task List
  • Be delivered or designed by a BCBA or BCBA-D
  • Include training on all task list areas: measurement, assessment, skill acquisition, behavior reduction, documentation, and professional conduct

Where to Get 40-Hour RBT Training

  • Employer-provided: Many ABA agencies provide free training to new hires
  • Online programs: Available from $50‚Äì$300 (e.g., Relias, BehaviorLive, ABA Rocks)
  • University programs: Some colleges offer RBT training as continuing education

Training can be completed in person, online, or a combination of both. Most online programs take 1‚Äì3 weeks to complete.

Step 3: Pass the Competency Assessment

After completing training, you must pass a competency assessment conducted by a BCBA, BCaBA, or BCBA-D. This is a hands-on evaluation where you demonstrate your ability to perform RBT tasks.

The assessment covers all areas of the RBT Task List and uses a standardized form provided by the BACB. You must score "competent" on each item. Learn more in our RBT Competency Assessment guide.

Step 4: Complete Background Check

The BACB requires all RBT applicants to complete a criminal background check through their approved vendor. Key details:

  • Cost: approximately $100
  • Processing time: 1‚Äì3 weeks
  • The background check must be completed before you can sit for the exam
  • Certain criminal convictions may affect eligibility (the BACB reviews on a case-by-case basis)

Step 5: Pass the RBT Exam

The final step is passing the RBT certification exam, administered by Pearson VUE:

  • 85 multiple-choice questions (75 scored, 10 unscored pilot items)
  • 90-minute time limit
  • Available at Pearson VUE test centers or via online proctoring
  • Exam fee: $45
  • Pass rate: approximately 80%
  • Results are available immediately after completion

After Certification: What's Next?

Once you pass the exam, you're officially an RBT! Here's what comes next:

  • Maintain certification: Complete a renewal competency assessment annually and meet supervision requirements
  • Work under supervision: RBTs must work under the ongoing supervision of a BCBA (minimum 5% of hours worked)
  • Pursue career growth: Consider advancing to BCaBA or BCBA for higher pay and more responsibility

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become an RBT?

Most people complete the entire RBT certification process in 3‚Äì8 weeks. The 40-hour training takes 1‚Äì4 weeks depending on the program, the competency assessment takes 1‚Äì2 days, and exam scheduling typically takes 1‚Äì2 weeks.

How much does RBT certification cost?

Total costs range from $100‚Äì$500+. The BACB exam fee is $45, background check is approximately $100, and 40-hour training programs range from free (employer-provided) to $300+. Many ABA agencies cover all certification costs for new hires.

Do you need a degree to become an RBT?

No. The minimum education requirement for RBT certification is a high school diploma or equivalent (GED). No college degree is required.

Can you take the RBT exam online?

Yes. The RBT exam can be taken at a Pearson VUE testing center or via online proctoring from home. Online testing requires a webcam, microphone, and a quiet, private room.

What happens if you fail the RBT exam?

You can retake the exam after a 30-day waiting period. You may attempt the exam up to 8 times within a 12-month cycle. Each retake costs $45.

What Is the Competency Assessment?

The RBT competency assessment is a direct observation evaluation where a qualified assessor watches you demonstrate the skills from the BACB RBT Task List (2nd Edition). It verifies that you can apply what you learned in your 40-hour training.

There are two types:

  • Initial competency assessment: Required before you can take the RBT exam
  • Annual competency assessment: Required each year to renew your certification

Who Conducts It?

The assessment must be conducted by one of the following:

  • BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst)
  • BCaBA (Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst) ‚Äî under BCBA supervision
  • BCBA-D (BCBA with doctoral designation)

The assessor does not need to be your ongoing supervisor, but they often are. If your employer provides an RBT training program, they'll typically arrange the competency assessment as well.

Skills Tested

The competency assessment covers all major areas of the RBT Task List:

Assessment Format

The assessor will use the BACB's standardized competency assessment form, which includes specific tasks you must demonstrate. Here's what to expect:

  • The assessment typically takes 1‚Äì3 hours
  • You may demonstrate skills with a real client or through role-play with the assessor
  • The assessor observes and rates each skill as "competent" or "not competent"
  • You may be asked to explain your reasoning for certain procedures
  • The assessor may create scenarios and ask you to respond appropriately

How It's Scored

Each item on the assessment is scored as either:

  • Competent: You demonstrated the skill correctly
  • Not Competent: You need additional training on this skill

You must be rated "competent" on every item to pass. If you're rated "not competent" on any items, your assessor will provide feedback and you'll need additional practice before being reassessed on those specific skills.

How to Prepare

1. Review the RBT Task List

Download the RBT Task List 2nd Edition from the BACB website and make sure you can explain and demonstrate every item.

2. Practice Skills with a Partner

Role-play with a colleague or classmate. Practice running discrete trials, taking data, implementing prompts, and using reinforcement.

3. Know Your Data Collection Methods

Be prepared to demonstrate:

  • Frequency/event recording ‚Äî counting occurrences of behavior
  • Duration recording ‚Äî timing how long a behavior lasts
  • Latency recording ‚Äî measuring time between instruction and response
  • Interval recording ‚Äî whole interval, partial interval, momentary time sampling
  • Permanent product recording ‚Äî measuring tangible outcomes

4. Master Prompting Hierarchies

Know the different prompt types and when to use them:

  • Full physical prompt ‚Äî hand-over-hand guidance
  • Partial physical prompt ‚Äî light touch or nudge
  • Model prompt ‚Äî demonstrating the correct response
  • Verbal prompt ‚Äî giving a verbal hint or instruction
  • Gestural prompt ‚Äî pointing or gesturing
  • Visual prompt ‚Äî picture cues or written instructions

5. Understand Reinforcement Principles

Be ready to demonstrate delivering reinforcement correctly ‚Äî immediately, consistently, and contingent on the target behavior. Know the difference between positive/negative reinforcement and punishment.

6. Review Professional Conduct

Know the basics of the BACB Ethics Code as it applies to RBTs ‚Äî confidentiality, professional boundaries, mandatory reporting, and working within your scope of competence.

Annual Renewal Assessment

The annual competency assessment for RBT renewal follows the same format as the initial assessment:

  • Same standardized form from the BACB
  • Same requirement to be rated "competent" on all items
  • Must be completed by a BCBA, BCaBA, or BCBA-D
  • Must be completed within your certification cycle (before your expiration date)

Many RBTs complete their annual assessment during a regular supervision session with their BCBA.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the RBT competency assessment?

The RBT competency assessment is a hands-on evaluation where a BCBA, BCaBA, or BCBA-D directly observes you demonstrating the skills outlined in the BACB RBT Task List. It is required for both initial certification and annual renewal.

How long does the RBT competency assessment take?

The assessment typically takes 1‚Äì3 hours to complete, depending on the assessor's approach and whether you need to demonstrate skills with a real client or through role-play.

Can you fail the RBT competency assessment?

Yes. If you are rated "not competent" on any item, you will need additional training and a reassessment on those items. There is no limit to how many times you can be reassessed.

Who can conduct the RBT competency assessment?

The competency assessment must be conducted by a BCBA, BCaBA (under BCBA supervision), or BCBA-D. The assessor does not need to be your ongoing supervisor, though they often are.

Can the competency assessment be done virtually?

The BACB allows competency assessments via telehealth/video conferencing as long as the assessor can clearly observe the RBT demonstrating skills. Check the BACB website for the most current policies.

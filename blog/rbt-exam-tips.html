15 RBT Exam Tips You Need to Know Before Test Day

üìÖ February 2025 ‚è± 10 min read üéì Exam Day

You've done the studying. You know your reinforcement from your punishment. But the exam itself has its own quirks. These 15 tips from people who already passed will give you the edge on test day.

Before the Exam

1 Book your exam date FIRST

Schedule your exam before you feel "ready." Having a fixed date creates urgency and prevents endless procrastination. Most people need 2-4 weeks of study ‚Äî not 2-4 months.

2 Take at least 2 full mock exams

Don't just do practice questions ‚Äî take full 85-question timed exams. This builds stamina and teaches you pacing. If you're consistently scoring 80%+, you're ready.

3 Focus on Sections C and F

Skill Acquisition (28%) and Professional Conduct (24%) make up over half the exam. If you're short on time, prioritize these. Ethics questions are often the trickiest.

4 Don't cram the night before

Your brain consolidates information during sleep. Do a light 20-minute review at most, then relax. Watch something fun. Go to bed early. Cramming the night before causes more harm than good.

5 Prepare your logistics in advance

Know exactly where the testing center is, what ID you need (two forms, one photo), and how long it takes to get there. Arrive 30 minutes early. Remove one source of stress before it starts.

During the Exam

6 Read every question TWICE

The RBT exam loves keywords like "best," "first," "most likely," and "least appropriate." These words completely change the answer. Read carefully before looking at the choices.

7 Eliminate wrong answers first

You can usually rule out 1-2 obviously wrong answers immediately. This turns a 1-in-4 guess into a 1-in-2 guess. Even if you're unsure, elimination dramatically improves your odds.

8 Flag and move on

If a question stumps you for more than 60 seconds, flag it and move on. Come back to it at the end with fresh eyes. Don't let one hard question steal time from three easy ones.

9 Trust your first answer

Research consistently shows that your first instinct is usually correct. Only change an answer if you have a clear, specific reason ‚Äî not just anxiety. "I feel like it might be C" is not a reason to change from B.

10 For ethics questions: protect the client

When in doubt on a professional conduct question, pick the answer that best protects the client's welfare, maintains boundaries, and follows your BCBA's guidance. "Consult your supervisor" is usually a safe bet.

11 Watch for "absolutes"

Answers with words like "always," "never," "all," or "none" are usually wrong. ABA is nuanced ‚Äî there are almost always exceptions. Answers with "usually," "typically," or "most likely" tend to be more accurate.

12 Manage your time

85 questions in 90 minutes = about 1 minute per question. Check in at question 40 ‚Äî you should be around the 45-minute mark. If you're falling behind, pick up the pace on easier questions.

Managing Test Anxiety

13 Breathe. Literally.

If you feel panic rising, stop and take 3 slow, deep breaths. 4 seconds in, hold for 4, out for 6. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and actually calms your brain. It's not woo-woo ‚Äî it's physiology.

14 Remember: 80% of people pass

The RBT exam is challenging but very passable. You don't need a perfect score ‚Äî you need roughly 68%. That means you can get nearly a third of the questions wrong and still pass. Breathe.

15 You can retake it

If the worst happens, you can retake the exam. It's $50 and a few weeks of extra studying. It's not the end of the world. Ironically, knowing you can retake it often reduces anxiety enough to help you pass the first time.

üí° The #1 Tip: Preparation is the best anxiety reducer. If you've gone through the flashcards, taken practice quizzes, and scored 80%+ on mock exams, you are ready. Trust the process.

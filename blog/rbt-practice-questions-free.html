Section A: Foundations (Questions 1-8)

1. What is the primary goal of Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA)?

  1. To diagnose behavioral disorders
  2. To understand and improve socially significant behaviors
  3. To prescribe medication for behavior problems
  4. To provide counseling services
‚úì Answer: B. ABA focuses on understanding and improving socially significant behaviors using evidence-based techniques. RBTs do not diagnose, prescribe medication, or provide counseling.

2. Which of the following is an example of a socially significant behavior?

  1. A child tapping their fingers on a desk
  2. A child learning to request items using words
  3. A child looking out a window
  4. A child humming quietly
‚úì Answer: B. Socially significant behaviors are those that meaningfully improve the individual's life. Learning to communicate needs (manding) is socially significant because it increases independence.

3. What are the three components of the "three-term contingency"?

  1. Stimulus, response, consequence
  2. Antecedent, behavior, consequence
  3. Setting, behavior, outcome
  4. Prompt, response, reinforcement
‚úì Answer: B. The three-term contingency (ABC) consists of the Antecedent (what happens before), the Behavior, and the Consequence (what happens after). This is a foundational concept in ABA.

4. Positive reinforcement involves:

  1. Removing something to increase behavior
  2. Adding something to decrease behavior
  3. Adding something to increase behavior
  4. Removing something to decrease behavior
‚úì Answer: C. Positive reinforcement = adding a stimulus after a behavior that increases the future likelihood of that behavior. Example: giving a sticker after a child completes a task.

5. Which of the following best describes negative reinforcement?

  1. Punishing a behavior
  2. Removing an aversive stimulus to increase behavior
  3. Ignoring a behavior
  4. Adding an aversive stimulus to decrease behavior
‚úì Answer: B. Negative reinforcement involves removing something unpleasant to increase behavior. "Negative" means removal, "reinforcement" means the behavior increases. Example: turning off a loud alarm when a child sits down.

6. An RBT should ALWAYS:

  1. Design their own behavior intervention plans
  2. Work under the supervision of a BCBA
  3. Provide diagnoses to families
  4. Modify programs without consulting their supervisor
‚úì Answer: B. RBTs must always work under the supervision of a BCBA or BCaBA. They do not design interventions, provide diagnoses, or modify programs independently.

7. What does the term "extinction" mean in ABA?

  1. A species dying out
  2. Withholding reinforcement for a previously reinforced behavior
  3. Punishing a behavior until it stops
  4. Gradually fading prompts
‚úì Answer: B. Extinction is the process of no longer providing reinforcement for a behavior that was previously reinforced. Over time, the behavior decreases.

8. Which schedule of reinforcement provides reinforcement after every correct response?

  1. Variable ratio
  2. Fixed interval
  3. Continuous reinforcement (CRF)
  4. Variable interval
‚úì Answer: C. Continuous reinforcement (CRF) means reinforcing every single correct response. It's best for teaching new behaviors.
Section B: Assessment (Questions 9-16)

9. Which data collection method records whether a behavior occurred during a specific time interval?

  1. Frequency recording
  2. Duration recording
  3. Interval recording
  4. Latency recording
‚úì Answer: C. Interval recording divides an observation period into equal time intervals and records whether the behavior occurred during each interval.

10. A preference assessment is used to:

  1. Determine the function of a behavior
  2. Identify potential reinforcers for an individual
  3. Measure the rate of a behavior
  4. Assess the severity of a behavior
‚úì Answer: B. Preference assessments help identify items, activities, or stimuli that may function as reinforcers for a specific individual.

11. What is the difference between frequency and rate?

  1. They are the same thing
  2. Frequency is a count; rate is frequency divided by time
  3. Rate is a count; frequency is rate divided by time
  4. Frequency measures duration; rate measures latency
‚úì Answer: B. Frequency is simply the count of how many times a behavior occurs. Rate is frequency divided by the observation time (e.g., 10 responses per minute).

12. Duration recording measures:

  1. How many times a behavior occurs
  2. How long a behavior lasts
  3. How long before a behavior starts
  4. Whether a behavior occurred in an interval
‚úì Answer: B. Duration recording measures the total amount of time a behavior lasts from onset to offset.

13. Latency recording measures:

  1. How long a behavior lasts
  2. The time between a stimulus and the start of a behavior
  3. How many times a behavior occurs
  4. The intensity of a behavior
‚úì Answer: B. Latency is the time from when a stimulus is presented (e.g., an instruction) to when the behavior begins.

14. In a multiple stimulus without replacement (MSWO) preference assessment:

  1. All items are presented and the chosen item is replaced
  2. All items are presented and the chosen item is removed before the next trial
  3. Only two items are presented at a time
  4. Items are presented one at a time
‚úì Answer: B. In MSWO, all items are presented, the individual chooses one, and that item is removed from the array. This creates a hierarchy of preferences.

15. ABC data collection stands for:

  1. Assessment, Behavior, Charting
  2. Antecedent, Behavior, Consequence
  3. Analysis, Baseline, Condition
  4. Application, Behavior, Conclusion
‚úì Answer: B. ABC data collection records the Antecedent (what happened before), the Behavior, and the Consequence (what happened after) to help identify patterns and potential functions.

16. Permanent product recording is best used when:

  1. You need to observe the behavior in real-time
  2. The behavior leaves a tangible outcome that can be measured later
  3. The behavior occurs too quickly to count
  4. You need to measure the intensity of a behavior
‚úì Answer: B. Permanent product recording measures the tangible results of a behavior (e.g., completed worksheets, items placed in a bin) without needing to observe the behavior directly.

Section C: Skill Acquisition (Questions 17-30)

17. Discrete Trial Training (DTT) consists of:

  1. Unstructured play with reinforcement
  2. An instruction, response, consequence, and inter-trial interval
  3. A natural environment teaching approach
  4. Modeling without prompts
‚úì Answer: B. DTT is a structured teaching method with clear components: the discriminative stimulus (instruction), the learner's response, a consequence (reinforcement or correction), and a brief pause between trials.

18. Which prompt is MOST intrusive?

  1. Gestural
  2. Verbal
  3. Physical (hand-over-hand)
  4. Visual
‚úì Answer: C. Physical prompts (hand-over-hand guidance) are the most intrusive. The prompt hierarchy from most to least intrusive: physical ‚Üí model ‚Üí gestural ‚Üí verbal ‚Üí visual.

19. What is prompt fading?

  1. Adding more prompts over time
  2. Gradually reducing the level of assistance to promote independence
  3. Removing all prompts at once
  4. Switching from one prompt type to another
‚úì Answer: B. Prompt fading is the systematic reduction of prompts so the learner becomes more independent. The goal is for the behavior to occur without any prompts.

20. Chaining is used to teach:

  1. Single-step behaviors
  2. Complex behaviors broken into smaller steps
  3. Social skills only
  4. Academic concepts only
‚úì Answer: B. Chaining teaches complex, multi-step behaviors (like hand washing or getting dressed) by breaking them into individual steps linked in a sequence.

21. In forward chaining, you:

  1. Teach the last step first
  2. Teach all steps at once
  3. Teach the first step first and prompt through the rest
  4. Only reinforce the final step
‚úì Answer: C. In forward chaining, the learner independently completes the first step and is prompted through the remaining steps. Once the first step is mastered, the second step is taught independently, and so on.

22. Modeling as a prompt involves:

  1. Physically guiding the learner
  2. Demonstrating the desired behavior for the learner to imitate
  3. Pointing to the correct answer
  4. Telling the learner what to do
‚úì Answer: B. Modeling means demonstrating the target behavior so the learner can observe and imitate it.

23. Natural Environment Teaching (NET) differs from DTT because:

  1. It uses no reinforcement
  2. It takes place during naturally occurring activities and follows the learner's motivation
  3. It requires a clinical setting
  4. It only teaches language skills
‚úì Answer: B. NET uses the learner's natural motivation and interests within everyday activities. It's more flexible and child-led compared to the structured format of DTT.

24. Generalization refers to:

  1. Performing a behavior only in the training setting
  2. Performing a learned behavior across different settings, people, or materials
  3. Forgetting a previously learned skill
  4. Learning a new behavior from scratch
‚úì Answer: B. Generalization occurs when a learner demonstrates a skill in settings, with people, or using materials that are different from those used during training.

25. A token economy is an example of:

  1. Punishment
  2. Extinction
  3. Conditioned reinforcement
  4. Unconditioned reinforcement
‚úì Answer: C. Tokens are conditioned (learned) reinforcers that are exchanged for backup reinforcers. They gain their reinforcing value through pairing with preferred items or activities.

26. Shaping involves:

  1. Reinforcing successive approximations of a target behavior
  2. Teaching all steps of a behavior at once
  3. Using physical prompts only
  4. Punishing incorrect responses
‚úì Answer: A. Shaping reinforces behaviors that are progressively closer to the target behavior. It's used when the target behavior doesn't yet exist in the learner's repertoire.

27. A discriminative stimulus (SD) signals that:

  1. Punishment is available
  2. Reinforcement is available for a specific behavior
  3. No reinforcement is available
  4. The session is over
‚úì Answer: B. An SD signals that reinforcement is available if the correct behavior is performed. Example: a teacher saying "touch red" is the SD for the learner to touch the red card.

28. Differential reinforcement of alternative behavior (DRA) involves:

  1. Reinforcing the problem behavior on a different schedule
  2. Reinforcing an appropriate alternative behavior while withholding reinforcement for the problem behavior
  3. Punishing the problem behavior
  4. Reinforcing any behavior except the problem behavior
‚úì Answer: B. DRA reinforces a specific, appropriate alternative behavior that serves the same function as the problem behavior, while placing the problem behavior on extinction.

29. Task analysis is:

  1. A type of data collection
  2. Breaking a complex skill into smaller, teachable steps
  3. A type of preference assessment
  4. A method of punishment
‚úì Answer: B. A task analysis breaks down a complex behavior into its component steps. It's used in chaining procedures to teach multi-step skills.

30. Maintenance of a behavior means:

  1. The behavior only occurs with prompts
  2. The behavior continues to occur after training has ended
  3. The behavior occurs in new settings
  4. The behavior increases in frequency
‚úì Answer: B. Maintenance refers to the continuation of a learned behavior over time after formal training and reinforcement have been reduced or stopped.
Section D: Behavior Reduction (Questions 31-38)

31. The four functions of behavior are:

  1. Attention, escape, tangible, sensory
  2. Positive, negative, punishment, extinction
  3. Social, academic, behavioral, emotional
  4. Frequency, duration, intensity, latency
‚úì Answer: A. The four functions of behavior (SEAT/ACES) are: Sensory/Automatic, Escape, Attention, and Tangible. Every behavior serves at least one of these functions.

32. A child screams every time they're asked to do math. The teacher removes the math worksheet. The screaming likely serves the function of:

  1. Attention
  2. Escape
  3. Tangible
  4. Sensory
‚úì Answer: B. The child screams (behavior) and the demand is removed (consequence). Since the behavior results in removal of an aversive task, the function is escape/avoidance.

33. Extinction burst refers to:

  1. A gradual decrease in behavior
  2. A temporary increase in the behavior when reinforcement is first withheld
  3. The behavior stopping immediately
  4. A new behavior appearing
‚úì Answer: B. An extinction burst is a temporary increase in the frequency, duration, or intensity of a behavior when reinforcement is first discontinued. This is normal and expected.

34. Positive punishment involves:

  1. Removing something to decrease behavior
  2. Adding something to decrease behavior
  3. Adding something to increase behavior
  4. Removing something to increase behavior
‚úì Answer: B. Positive punishment = adding a stimulus that decreases the future likelihood of the behavior. "Positive" means adding, "punishment" means behavior decreases.

35. An RBT should implement behavior reduction procedures:

  1. Based on their own judgment
  2. Only as outlined in the behavior intervention plan (BIP) written by the BCBA
  3. Only when the behavior is dangerous
  4. After consulting with the client's family
‚úì Answer: B. RBTs must follow the behavior intervention plan as written by the supervising BCBA. They should never modify procedures or create their own interventions.

36. Differential reinforcement of other behavior (DRO) involves:

  1. Reinforcing a specific alternative behavior
  2. Reinforcing the absence of the target behavior for a set time period
  3. Reinforcing every behavior the person does
  4. Using punishment for the target behavior
‚úì Answer: B. DRO provides reinforcement when the target (problem) behavior has NOT occurred during a specified time interval. The "other" refers to any behavior other than the target.

37. Antecedent interventions are strategies that:

  1. Occur after the behavior
  2. Are implemented before the behavior occurs to prevent it
  3. Only involve punishment
  4. Are only used in school settings
‚úì Answer: B. Antecedent interventions modify the environment or conditions before a behavior occurs to prevent or reduce the likelihood of problem behavior. Examples: visual schedules, choices, priming.

38. Functional communication training (FCT) teaches the individual to:

  1. Communicate only through sign language
  2. Use an appropriate communicative response to replace the problem behavior
  3. Stop communicating when frustrated
  4. Use problem behavior to communicate
‚úì Answer: B. FCT teaches a replacement communication response that serves the same function as the problem behavior. For example, teaching a child to say "break please" instead of screaming to escape a task.
Section E: Documentation and Reporting (Questions 39-44)

39. Session notes should include:

  1. Only positive behaviors
  2. Objective descriptions of behavior and data collected
  3. Your personal opinions about the client
  4. Information about other clients
‚úì Answer: B. Session notes should be objective, factual, and include data collected during the session. Avoid subjective language, opinions, or information about other clients.

40. When graphing data, the independent variable is typically placed on the:

  1. Y-axis (vertical)
  2. X-axis (horizontal)
  3. Title
  4. Legend
‚úì Answer: B. The independent variable (usually sessions or time) is plotted on the x-axis. The dependent variable (the behavior being measured) is on the y-axis.

41. An RBT notices a sudden change in a client's behavior. They should:

  1. Ignore it
  2. Document it and report it to their BCBA supervisor
  3. Call the client's parents immediately
  4. Change the intervention on their own
‚úì Answer: B. Any significant changes in behavior should be documented objectively and reported to the supervising BCBA, who will determine if any changes to the plan are needed.

42. Client records and data should be:

  1. Shared freely with other staff
  2. Kept confidential and stored securely
  3. Posted on social media for training purposes
  4. Discussed with friends and family of the RBT
‚úì Answer: B. Client records are confidential. They must be stored securely and only shared with authorized individuals involved in the client's care.

43. When reporting data, an RBT should:

  1. Estimate the data if they forgot to record it
  2. Only report data that shows improvement
  3. Report data accurately and honestly
  4. Round numbers up to make the data look better
‚úì Answer: C. Data integrity is essential. Always report data accurately and honestly. Fabricating, estimating, or selectively reporting data is unethical and could harm the client.

44. A line graph showing a client's progress over time is called a:

  1. Bar graph
  2. Pie chart
  3. Cumulative record
  4. Line graph / time-series graph
‚úì Answer: D. A line graph (or time-series graph) is the most common way to display behavioral data over time in ABA, showing trends, patterns, and the effects of interventions.
Section F: Professional Conduct (Questions 45-50)

45. An RBT's primary obligation is to:

  1. The client's insurance company
  2. The client and their wellbeing
  3. The supervising BCBA
  4. The employer
‚úì Answer: B. The RBT's primary obligation is always to the client. While RBTs work under BCBA supervision and follow employer policies, the client's wellbeing comes first.

46. Dual relationships between an RBT and a client should be:

  1. Encouraged to build rapport
  2. Avoided to maintain professional boundaries
  3. Only a concern for BCBAs
  4. Acceptable if the client initiates them
‚úì Answer: B. Dual relationships (e.g., being a client's friend, family member's therapist, or having a financial relationship) should be avoided to maintain objectivity and professional boundaries.

47. If an RBT suspects a client is being abused, they should:

  1. Confront the suspected abuser
  2. Follow mandatory reporting laws and report to the appropriate authorities
  3. Wait to see if it happens again
  4. Only tell their BCBA
‚úì Answer: B. RBTs are mandatory reporters. If abuse or neglect is suspected, it must be reported to the appropriate authorities (e.g., child protective services) as required by law. Also inform your supervisor.

48. How often must an RBT renew their certification?

  1. Every 6 months
  2. Every year
  3. Every 2 years
  4. Every 5 years
‚úì Answer: B. RBT certification must be renewed annually. Renewal requirements include a competency assessment and ongoing supervision documentation.

49. An RBT receives a gift from a client's family. The BEST response is to:

  1. Accept it graciously
  2. Politely decline and explain professional boundaries
  3. Accept it but don't tell anyone
  4. Ask for a different gift
‚úì Answer: B. RBTs should politely decline gifts to maintain professional boundaries. Accepting gifts can create a dual relationship and compromise objectivity.

50. An RBT should maintain confidentiality by:

  1. Discussing clients only with authorized individuals in private settings
  2. Sharing funny stories about clients with coworkers during lunch
  3. Posting client progress on social media (without names)
  4. Discussing clients in the waiting room
‚úì Answer: A. Client information should only be discussed with authorized individuals (BCBA, team members) in private settings. Even without names, sharing stories or details about clients in public settings or on social media violates confidentiality.

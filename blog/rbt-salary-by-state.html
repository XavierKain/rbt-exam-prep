Factors That Affect RBT Pay

1. Geographic Location

As the table above shows, location is the single biggest factor. States with higher costs of living, larger populations of children with autism, and stronger insurance mandates for ABA therapy tend to pay more.

2. Work Setting

RBTs work in several settings, each with different pay structures:

  • In-home ABA therapy: Often the most common. Pay ranges from $16‚Äì$25/hour.
  • Clinic-based ABA: Typically offers more consistent hours and may include benefits. $17‚Äì$24/hour.
  • Schools: School-based RBT positions may offer lower hourly rates but include benefits like summers off, health insurance, and retirement plans.
  • Hospitals and residential facilities: Less common but may offer the highest hourly rates ($19‚Äì$28/hour).

3. Experience Level

Experience significantly impacts RBT compensation:

  • 0‚Äì1 years: $15‚Äì$18/hour (entry-level)
  • 1‚Äì3 years: $17‚Äì$21/hour
  • 3‚Äì5 years: $19‚Äì$24/hour
  • 5+ years: $21‚Äì$28/hour

4. Employer Type

Large national ABA companies (like Trumpet Behavioral Health, Centria Autism, or Behavior Analysis Inc.) may offer different compensation packages than small local practices. Larger companies often provide better benefits; smaller companies may offer higher hourly rates.

5. Specialization and Additional Certifications

RBTs who pursue additional training ‚Äî such as crisis intervention certification, specialization in early intervention, or bilingual skills ‚Äî can command higher pay.

How to Increase Your RBT Salary

If you're looking to earn more as an RBT, consider these strategies:

  1. Gain experience. Even 1‚Äì2 years of hands-on ABA therapy experience can significantly increase your pay.
  2. Pursue additional certifications. Crisis prevention (CPI), CPR/First Aid, or specialized ABA training adds value.
  3. Work toward becoming a BCaBA or BCBA. The biggest salary jump comes from advancing your education. BCaBAs earn $45,000‚Äì$55,000, and BCBAs earn $60,000‚Äì$90,000+ annually. Learn more in our RBT vs BCBA comparison.
  4. Negotiate. Many RBTs don't negotiate their starting salary. Research the going rate in your area and ask for what you're worth.
  5. Consider relocation. Moving to a higher-paying state or metro area can increase your income by 20‚Äì40%.
  6. Be bilingual. ABA agencies serving diverse populations often pay a premium for bilingual RBTs, especially Spanish-English speakers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average RBT salary in the US?

The average RBT salary in the United States is approximately $37,710 per year ($18.13/hour) as of 2025, based on BLS data for healthcare support occupations in behavior analysis settings.

Which state pays RBTs the most?

New Jersey, California, and Massachusetts consistently rank as the highest-paying states for RBTs, with average salaries ranging from $43,000 to $48,000 per year.

Do RBTs get benefits?

Many full-time RBT positions include benefits such as health insurance, paid time off, and continuing education reimbursement. Benefits vary by employer ‚Äî ABA agencies, schools, and hospitals each offer different packages.

Can RBTs make more than $50,000 a year?

Yes. RBTs in high-cost-of-living areas (especially California, New Jersey, and the Northeast), those with several years of experience, or those working overtime can earn over $50,000 annually.

Is being an RBT worth it financially?

For a role that requires only a high school diploma and a 40-hour training program, RBT certification offers competitive pay with strong job security and growth potential. It's also an excellent stepping stone to higher-paying careers like BCaBA or BCBA.

What Is the RBT Exam?

The RBT exam is a computer-based certification test administered by the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB). It tests your knowledge of applied behavior analysis (ABA) concepts as they relate to the role of a Registered Behavior Technician.

To be eligible, you need:

  • A high school diploma or equivalent
  • Completion of a 40-hour RBT training program
  • A background check
  • A completed competency assessment

Exam Format & Structure

Here's exactly what you'll face on test day:

  • 85 multiple-choice questions (75 scored, 10 unscored pilot questions)
  • 90-minute time limit
  • 4 answer choices per question
  • Questions are based on the RBT Task List (2nd Edition)
  • You need approximately 68% to pass (the exact cut score varies)
üí° Key Takeaway: You don't know which 10 questions are unscored, so treat every question as if it counts. Don't rush through any of them.

Understanding the RBT Task List (2nd Edition)

The RBT Task List (2nd Edition) is your exam blueprint. Every question comes from one of these sections:

  • A. Foundations (12%) ‚Äî ABA principles, ethics, professional conduct
  • B. Assessment (12%) ‚Äî Data collection, preference assessments
  • C. Skill Acquisition (28%) ‚Äî Teaching procedures, prompting, reinforcement
  • D. Behavior Reduction (12%) ‚Äî Function-based interventions, extinction
  • E. Documentation and Reporting (12%) ‚Äî Session notes, graphing data
  • F. Professional Conduct and Scope of Practice (24%) ‚Äî Ethics, boundaries, supervision

Notice that Skill Acquisition (28%) and Professional Conduct (24%) make up over half the exam. Focus your study time accordingly.

The Best Study Strategy

After talking to hundreds of RBT candidates, here's the study approach that works best:

1. Start with Flashcards (Week 1-2)

Go through the entire task list using flashcards. Don't try to memorize everything ‚Äî focus on understanding concepts. Mark cards as "know it," "learning," or "need review."

2. Practice Questions (Week 2-3)

Switch to multiple-choice practice questions. The goal isn't just getting the right answer ‚Äî it's understanding why the wrong answers are wrong. Read every explanation.

3. Mock Exams (Week 3-4)

Take full 85-question practice exams under timed conditions. This builds your stamina and helps you manage time on test day. Aim for 80%+ consistently before scheduling your exam.

4. Review Weak Areas (Ongoing)

After each practice exam, identify the sections where you scored lowest. Go back to the flashcards for those topics. Repeat until there are no weak spots.

üí° Pro Tip: Study in 25-minute focused sessions (Pomodoro technique). Short, intense sessions beat long, unfocused ones every time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Only reading notes ‚Äî Passive reading doesn't work. You need active recall (flashcards + practice questions).
  2. Ignoring ethics questions ‚Äî Professional conduct is 24% of the exam. Don't skip it.
  3. Not timing practice exams ‚Äî You have 90 minutes for 85 questions. That's about 1 minute per question. Practice under pressure.
  4. Studying too much the day before ‚Äî Rest your brain. Light review only.
  5. Changing answers ‚Äî Your first instinct is usually right. Only change an answer if you're certain it's wrong.

Exam Day Tips

  • Arrive 30 minutes early ‚Äî You'll need time for check-in and ID verification.
  • Bring two forms of ID ‚Äî One must be a government-issued photo ID.
  • Read each question twice ‚Äî Look for keywords like "best," "first," "most likely."
  • Flag and skip ‚Äî If a question stumps you, flag it and come back. Don't waste time.
  • Trust your preparation ‚Äî If you've been scoring 80%+ on practice exams, you're ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many questions do you need to get right to pass the RBT exam?

The exact passing score varies, but you generally need around 68% correct on the scored questions (approximately 51 out of 75 scored questions). Since 10 questions are unscored pilot items, aim for at least 58 correct out of 85 total.

How long should I study for the RBT exam?

Most candidates study for 2 to 4 weeks, spending 1-2 hours per day. If you're already working as an RBT under supervision, you may need less time since you're applying concepts daily.

Can I retake the RBT exam if I fail?

Yes. You can retake the exam up to 8 times in a 12-month period. There's a waiting period between attempts and you'll need to pay the exam fee again ($50).

Is the RBT exam hard?

The RBT exam is challenging but very passable with proper preparation. The pass rate is around 80%. Most people who fail didn't study enough or focused on the wrong areas. Use the task list as your study guide and you'll be fine.
